Frequently Asked Questions

What is motor truck cargo insurance?

Motor truck cargo insurance protects the freight you're hauling from loss or damage. It's typically required by shippers and brokers, with coverage limits ranging from $100K to $250K or more depending on commodity.

Do I need general liability as a trucking company?

Yes, general liability protects your business from third-party claims not covered by your auto policy. This includes slip-and-fall accidents at your premises, damage during loading operations, and other business-related incidents.

What does reefer breakdown insurance cover?

Reefer breakdown covers spoilage of refrigerated cargo due to mechanical failure of your cooling equipment. This is essential if you haul temperature-sensitive goods like food, pharmaceuticals, or flowers.

How much cargo insurance do I need?

Coverage requirements vary by shipper and commodity. Most require minimum $100K, but high-value goods may require $250K or more. Your insurance agent can help determine appropriate limits based on your typical loads.
