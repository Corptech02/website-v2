Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between property and casualty insurance?

Property insurance covers physical assets like buildings and equipment, while casualty insurance covers liability for injuries or damage you cause to others.

Do I need both general and professional liability?

It depends on your business. General liability covers common risks, while professional liability is essential if you provide professional services or advice.

What are typical liability limits?

Common limits range from $1 million to $5 million per occurrence, but higher limits are available through umbrella policies for additional protection.

How are casualty insurance premiums calculated?

Premiums are based on factors including your industry, business size, claims history, coverage limits, and risk management practices.
