Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need workers' compensation for my trucking company?

Most states require workers' comp if you have employees. Requirements vary by state, but generally apply to companies with one or more employees. Owner-operators without employees may not need it but should consider occupational accident coverage.

What's the difference between workers' comp and occupational accident?

Workers' comp is state-mandated for employees and provides guaranteed benefits. Occupational accident is a private insurance option popular with owner-operators and independent contractors, offering similar protection without state requirements.

Does commercial property insurance cover my trucks?

No, commercial property covers your buildings, terminal facilities, office equipment, and business property. Your trucks and trailers need separate commercial auto physical damage coverage.

Why do I need non-trucking liability as an owner-operator?

Non-trucking liability (also called bobtail insurance) covers you when not under dispatch - driving home, personal errands, or moving to pick up a load. Your motor carrier's policy typically doesn't cover these situations.
